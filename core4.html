Variables A storage location containing some quantity of information, its value, that is liable to change Your age (in years) is a variable because you have a definite age at each moment and your age changes every year.
Loops A series of instructions that is continually repeated until some condition is reached. Breathing is like a looped action, because you're (almost) always breathing until you die.
Conditionals A feature that performs different actions depending on a programmer-specified Boolean (true-false) value. In the United States, some laws apply differently to you depending on how old you are. For example, if you're under 21, you can't legally drink alcohol, meaning you may be punished if you do. If you're 21 or over, however, it's all good.
Functions A series of code that performs an action. Chewing is a function. It performs a specified series of actions and can be repeated many times.
