Day 1: Intro to Robotics

I used to think robots had to have some characteristics of or be associated with a living thing, but I don't think that's true in general. At least, not according to the class definition of robot.

I'm most excited about artificial intelligence. Right now I'm interested in teaching robots to process and speak human language, and to a lesser extent, to move and emote like humans.

I'm nervous about self-driving cars and developments like Toymail in that they might get hacked. I'm not really worried about self-driving cars crashing or breaking down, because eventually there must be trust in technology. When I board a plane, I'm trusting that the piloting systems won't break down horribly enough that the plane will crash or explode with a reasonable probability, because planes have been used for a while and we've worked out many of the bugs. Of course, I could die horribly, but the convenience outweighs the risk, so I choose to take the plane. I anticipate that the same will be true for self-driving cars, someday at least. Sure, the logic isn't exactly sound, but I worry too much in general, and I'm glad that there's at least one thing I'm not worried about.
